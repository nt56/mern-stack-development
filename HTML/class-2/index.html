Monkey D. Luffy, the beloved protagonist of the globally celebrated manga and anime series One Piece, is best known for his unwavering honesty, boundless optimism, and strong moral compass. Roronoa Zoro Vice-Captain of straw hat pirate ship

Zoro is a swordsman , which is something that immediately attracted me to his character. Swords are easily the coolest weapons, it ’s why I don’t mind the amount in Smash, and beyond that I also love Samurai and the Bushido code, which while Zoro isn’t a Samurai, he does embody the Bushido principles of honour and loyalty. Just ask Vivi, she knew what was up. But Zoro’s not just your run-of-the-mill swordsman, he’s the most unsanitary swordsman in anime, with his santoryu fighting style, two in hand and one in mouth. I love his three sword style, Oda managed to find a way to make Zoro immediately distinct from the thousands of other sword-wielders in the medium, and it instills Zoro’s character with Oda’s creative charm from right out of the gate. And I love how much his fighting style is represented throughout his design. Good stuff like his haramaki showcasing a more traditional Japanese fashion sensibility, especially fitting since it was originally used during wartimes, but I more so appreciate how much three’s influence his style. There’s obviously his swords, can’t wield them if he doesn’t carry them but additional touches like his earrings having three gold dangles or his colour palette usually being limited to three colours, helps the design stand out a lot.
Granted, in manga the latter point isn’t as noticeably effective, and his later outfits don’t handle it as well, but in his original design, the colour placement is on point. When it comes to his attire, he only has three colours, white, black, and green.
